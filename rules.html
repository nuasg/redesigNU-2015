Rules!

Contact us if you have any question or follow our Facebook event page for questions

PARTICIPATION

All participants must be full-time or part-time University students, undergraduate or graduate student, during Spring 2015 except where permission is explicitly given.

TEAMS

Teams must consist of between 1 and 6 participants. Teams must submit a list of members upon submitting their project on Monday, June 1st. Any modifications after that must be approved by organizers. You can, by all means go solo, but we encourage you to work in groups!

SUBMISSIONS

Each team is permitted one submission that must be received by 12:00pm Monday, June 1st. Code submitted can be produced from any time period. Previous projects or using old code written is allowed for this event. Third party libraries are allowed without notice provided that they are publicly available to any participant. Each submission must contain instructions of how to run and a description of your project.

JUDGING CRITERIA

Teamsâ€™ applications will be judged on several criteria including usefulness to students, functionality, visual design, innovation, software development, and creativity. More details about the judging process will be released soon. The judging panel will determine a winner, runner-up, and a list of finalists. The Northwestern community will then vote for the winners for several categories.

OWNERSHIP

Any submitted projects are owned by the team that created it. ASG will not take ownership of your project without your permission. Winners may have an opportunity to work with ASG to implement their idea.
